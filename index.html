Apps & tools that fight doomscrolling

I like building systems that make focus easier by design – not by “just have more discipline” advice.

FocusLimit

Limit reels instead of pretending you’ll quit them.

FocusLimit tracks how many short-form videos you watch across apps and lets you set hard caps. It doesn’t judge you for opening Instagram – it just stops the “one more swipe” spiral.

Status
In planning / early build
Platforms
iOS · Android · optional browser extension
Tech stack
Android (Kotlin/Java + Accessibility Services), iOS (Swift + Screen Time API), backend optional (Supabase)
Business model
Freemium + subscription, potential affiliates

Designed for realistic behaviour: people will scroll – the goal is to keep it inside healthy limits.

Habit Garden

A 3D garden that grows with your habits.

Habit Garden is a long-term heart project: a stylised 3D garden where every habit unlocks growth. Less streak anxiety, more visible progress over seasons.

Status
Vision & design doc · long-term build
Visual direction
Unity (URP), low-poly stylised look, inspired by mobile AAA games
Architecture
Hybrid iOS app (SwiftUI + embedded Unity), offline-first with EU backend
Features
XP & levels, daily deals, friend codes, privacy by design

Built slowly and intentionally – this is a universe I want to live in myself, not just an MVP to flip.

Study & systems: keeping uni on track

Business Informatics at Uni Hamburg plus projects sounds chaotic. Study sprints and simple systems keep it raceable.

Core modules

  • Maths Analysis, linear algebra, proofs & induction as mental strength training.
  • CS Java, software development, BlueJ projects and fundamentals of computer science.
  • BWL Companies, business models and economic principles – the theory behind my projects.
  • HCI Human-computer interaction, usability and device design – perfect match for focus apps.

Study sprints

  • Pre-season

    Setting up calendars, blocking focus slots and defining minimum weekly outputs for math, CS and BWL.

  • Mid-season push

    Tight loops: lectures → notes → exercises → short recaps. Using simple dashboards to track where I’m falling behind early.

  • Exam weeks

    Clear priorities: fewer calls & less community work, more repetition sets and old exam tasks – no all-nighters if possible.

  • Off-season

    Post-mortem for each semester and adjusting systems: what worked, what was overkill, what can be automated or delegated.

How it all connects

The same principles show up everywhere: clear seasons, honest metrics and focus by design. Skool communities, FocusLimit and Habit Garden are all built around that idea.

If a setup doesn’t survive exam weeks or busy client sprints, it’s not a real system. On Track is where I test if ideas survive pressure.
