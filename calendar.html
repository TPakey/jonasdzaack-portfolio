Focus blocks like races

Instead of micro-managing every day, I group work into focus blocks. Each block has one clear priority and a “win condition”.

Study Block · Exam prep

3–4 weeks · heavy focus on maths, informatics & BWL

Deep work for uni: lectures, exercises, proofs, projects. Skool and products go into “maintenance mode” here to avoid context switching.

Community Block · Skool growth

2–3 weeks · funnels, launches, retention

Designing or optimising free-to-paid funnels, onboarding flows, leaderboards, DM-scripts and tracking. Usually where revenue jumps happen.

Build Block · Apps & Tools

1–2 weeks · product sprints

Shipping small but real pieces of FocusLimit, Habit Garden or new tools. Clear scope, clear done-definition, no endless refactors.

Light Block · Off-Track

1+ week · travel, F1, recharge

Gym, races, friends, maybe a trip. I still capture ideas and tighten systems, but output is intentionally lower so the next Race Season can be full send again.

What this calendar actually tells you

I’m a student and an operator at the same time. This calendar is my way of not lying to myself (or to you) about focus and availability.

Best time to reach out

If you want help with Skool communities or funnels, the best timing is during Race Season or Community Blocks – that’s when I actively build and optimise.

Exam & deep study phases

During Study Blocks my response time is slower. I still show up, but async and without long calls. Think of it as “safety car” instead of full race pace.
