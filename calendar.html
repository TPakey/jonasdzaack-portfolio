Season schedule: how the year actually feels

A simple board that maps months to focus modes. Less “perfect productivity system”, more realistic race calendar.

Pre-season
Jan – Mar Setup & routines

Getting into rhythm after breaks: setting up university structure, cleaning up Notion, planning app sprints and refreshing Skool systems.

  • Math / CS pre-work and recap sessions
  • Define FocusLimit milestones for the year
  • Plan community experiments & funnels
Race weeks
Exam phases & launches High intensity

Short, intense blocks with fewer experiments and more execution: exams at uni, launch windows for communities, big pushes for app features.

  • Exam sprints with clear study plans
  • Launch windows for Skool communities
  • Feature freezes so nothing new distracts
Mid-season push
Between exams Deep work projects

The quieter parts of the semester: perfect for deeper app work, long-term Skool systems and bigger experiments that don’t fit into a race week.

  • Implementing bigger FocusLimit features
  • Improving Skool onboarding & retention systems
  • Research & experiments (AI video detector, etc.)
Off-season
Breaks & travel Reset & reflection

Deliberate down-time: travel, smaller projects, reflection and refactoring. Less output, more thinking about what the next season should look like.

  • Short trips (Nord-/Ostsee, cities, maybe US / SA)
  • Light maintenance of communities & apps
  • Review what worked, what didn’t and why

Upcoming highlights

Not every date is here – only the milestones that actually change how I plan my focus.

Exams

Next exam phase

Math, CS and BWL blocks that define the intensity of the season. Study sprints are planned backwards from these dates.

  • Revise lecture notes & exercise sheets early
  • Lock time for deep study blocks in the calendar
  • Reduce new experiments & content during this window
FocusLimit

App milestones

FocusLimit has its own micro-season: prototype, first test users, then a more polished version if the idea holds up.

  • v0 concept & tracking logic nailed down
  • Internal test build with limited users
  • Decision point: ship, pivot or park
Communities

Skool community sprints

Short, focused pushes for communities I operate or help build: new funnels, events, content drops or launch series.

  • Planning 21-day growth sprints
  • Updating funnels & onboarding flows
  • Testing new engagement mechanics
Wildcard

Open slot for the unexpected

There’s always something that appears mid-season: a new collab, an idea, or a challenge. Keeping one slot unplanned avoids overloading the board.

  • Room for one bigger “surprise project”
  • Buffer so delays don’t crash the system
  • Flexibility without losing the overall rhythm
