OFF TRACK · LIFE

Life, F1 and experiments
between races.

On Track is where the systems live. Off Track is the rest of my world: F1, gaming, trips, perfumes, cooking and random ideas that might become products later – or just stay fun.

I don’t believe in “hustle 24/7”. Focus needs recovery, and this is where that recovery happens.

What lives off track

  • F1 & Lando – design inspiration, race weekends, 3D-helmet nerding.
  • Chill & everyday – gaming, gym, perfumes, cooking, hanging out.
  • Experiments – AI video origin detector, small prototypes, design tests.

These things don’t have to “justify themselves” – but they do feed how I design and operate systems.

F1 & Lando as a design language

I don’t want to be Lando. But his site set the bar for how playful, high-end and smooth a personal website can feel.

F1 & Web

Taking the vibe, not the code.

The Lando Norris site by It’s Off Brand is full of depth maps, 3D helmets and custom scroll logic. This portfolio isn’t copying it – it translates the vibe into my world: focus, communities and apps.

  • Visual overload, but controlled and performance-aware
  • Scroll as a story, not just a way to move down
  • Subtle 3D / tilt instead of heavy WebGL everywhere

My rule: inspiration is allowed, clones are not.

Motorsport metaphors

On Track vs Off Track, seasons, sprints, race weekends, pit stops – it’s not a gimmick. It’s how I structure my work and recovery.

Motion as a feature

Hover text, tilt cards, subtle loaders – motion should feel like feedback, not like an obstacle between you and the content.

Off track lifestyle: small things that add up

None of this is a “niche” or product. It’s just my normal life – but it shapes how I think about focus and energy.

Perfumes

Smelling good as a tiny life buff.

I like good scents – from Replica vibes to heavier, darker notes. It’s a small ritual that makes studying, calls and building feel more intentional.

Cooking & baking

Waffles, lasagne & slow evenings.

Cooking is one of the few things where I can’t doomscroll at the same time. It forces me off-screen and into a different pace.

Gym

Maintaining, not min-maxing.

Right now the gym is less about PRs and more about keeping a baseline. Strong enough to handle study + projects without burning out.

Trips

Day trips & future long-hauls.

From quick Nord-/Ostsee days to thinking about US or South America trips – changing the environment always resets my focus.

Gaming & chill: controlled escapism

I like games the same way I like social media: in the right dose, with some boundaries. No shame, just design.

Games as a conscious break.

I use games as a way to switch off my “operator brain” for a bit. No productivity hack, just play – but with time limits and some awareness so it doesn’t bleed into study time.

The same logic that goes into FocusLimit goes into how I think about gaming and scroll time: you can’t remove it, but you can put rails around it.

Side experiments that may go On Track later

Not every idea deserves a full product. But some need a sandbox. That’s what Off Track is for.

AI video origin detector

Is this video AI-generated or not?

Early idea for a tool that scans short-form videos and tries to detect if they’re AI-generated. The goal: more transparency for scrolling humans, less confusion.

  • Focus on short-form, not long YouTube docs
  • Simple UI, clear yes/maybe/no feedback
  • Built as a learning project on my Mac / Windows setup first

If it becomes useful and stable, it might move On Track as a real tool.

Design & motion tests

Trying things before shipping them.

Small experiments with motion, loaders, tilt effects and UI layouts – often inspired by high-end sites like Lando’s or Apple, but implemented in my own stack.

Some of these end up in this site, in Skool communities or inside apps. Others stay as unfinished playgrounds, which is fine.

Future wildcards

Ideas with no category yet.

Some notes don’t fit into “Skool”, “apps” or “uni” yet: maybe travel content, maybe different types of communities, maybe something completely offline.

Off Track keeps them visible without forcing them into a business case too early.
